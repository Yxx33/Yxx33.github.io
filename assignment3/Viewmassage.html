  • Temple of Heaven, formerly known as "Temple of Heaven and Earth", is located on the east side of Yongdingmen Street, Dongcheng District, Beijing. A place for the emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties to sacrifice heaven, pray for grain and pray for rain. It is the largest existing ancient sacrificial building complex in China. [3]. The Temple of Heaven was built in the 18th year of Yongle of the Ming Dynasty (1420), and the name of the Temple of Heaven was renamed "Temple of Heaven" in the ninth year of Ming Jiajing (1530). The Temple of Heaven is surrounded by two walls, divided into inner and outer altars. The main ancient buildings are concentrated in the inner altar, which is divided into the south and the north by an east-west partition wall, and there is a door in the partition wall. The inner altar is composed of three groups of ancient buildings: Qiqiu, Qigu altar and Zhai Palace. The Qigu altar is located in the north of the inner altar, the circular mound altar is located in the south of the inner altar, and the Zhai Palace is located in the west of the inner altar. A Danbi Bridge, which is 360 meters long, 28 meters wide and 2.5 meters high, connects the circular mound altar and the grain prayer altar. The inner altar is surrounded by East Tianmen, North Tianmen, West Tianmen, Guangli Gate, Zhaoheng Gate and Taiyuan Gate. Temple of Heaven has high historical value, scientific value and unique artistic value, but also has profound cultural connotations. [6]. On March 4, 1961, the Temple of Heaven was announced by the State Council of the people's Republic of China as the first batch of national key cultural relics protection units.
